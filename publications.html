Gut Microbiota Secondary Metabolites: Key Roles in GI Tract Cancers and Infectious Diseases

Muhammad Ajagbe et al.

This review examines how the gut microbiota produces secondary metabolites — including short-chain fatty acids, indoles, and bile acids — that modulate immune responses and epigenetic mechanisms. In gastrointestinal cancers (colorectal, liver, gastric), these metabolites can drive tumorigenesis through inflammation, DNA damage, and immune evasion, while also holding therapeutic promise by enhancing chemotherapy and immunotherapy responses. The review further explores their role in infectious disease resilience and calls for deeper research into microbiome-based therapeutic interventions.
