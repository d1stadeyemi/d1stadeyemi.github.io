Hiking Nature Early Morning

When Five Scientists Decided to Fight a Mountain at Dawn

It was 5:30 AM on a crisp November morning when someone — and we will never confirm who — decided that the best way to spend a Saturday was to drive from Knoxville to the Great Smoky Mountains and hike before the sun had fully committed to rising. Four friends, one shared questionable decision, and absolutely zero adequate preparation for the cold.

We arrived at the trailhead looking like a group of people who had googled "what to wear hiking" at midnight. Two of us wore hoodies. One wore actual hiking boots. The trail, unbothered by our confidence, was steep, foggy, and delightfully unforgiving.

Somewhere between the second and third steep incline, someone suggested we were "basically at the top." We were not. We were not even close. But the views that eventually rewarded our stubbornness — the Smokies stretching out in their misty, golden November glory — made every wheeze absolutely worth it.

We reached the summit, took the obligatory photos, ate granola bars like they were Michelin-star meals, and made a solemn group pact to "do this again soon." That pact has not yet been honoured. But the mountains are still there. Waiting.

"The mountain didn't break us. Mostly."
London, UK
April 7, 2025 Brothers' Trip London, United Kingdom
Royal History Travel

Two Brothers, One King, and an Absolutely Bewildering Amount of History

My brother and I arrived at Buckingham Palace on the morning of April 7th armed with nothing but enthusiasm, terrible map-reading skills, and the firm belief that we would somehow spot King Charles through a window. We did not. The King, wisely, was elsewhere. The guards, impressively, did not blink once despite our best efforts.

We stood at the gates like two tourists who had just discovered that palaces are, in fact, very large and very closed to the general public. We took photos anyway. Several of them. From every possible angle. The palace remained unmoved.

Next stop: the Natural History Museum — a building so grand it made us feel both intellectually inspired and personally inadequate within the first five minutes. We spent a suspicious amount of time at the dinosaur exhibition for two grown adults, and zero apologies are offered for that.

We finished the day at the Tower of London — where centuries of royal drama, imprisoned queens, and the Crown Jewels are casually on display between the gift shop and the café. We left mildly unsettled by how cheerfully they present all of that history, and thoroughly impressed by how much London manages to fit into one day.

"We came for the King. We stayed for the dinosaurs."
