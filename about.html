Background

I am a PhD student in Genomic Science and Technology at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, with a background spanning biotechnology, regulatory genomics, and large-scale metagenomics.

Research Experience

My work has focused on extracting biological structure from high-dimensional sequencing data. During my MSc at the American University in Cairo, I developed computational pipelines to process over 1 TB of metagenomic data, reconstructing 37 metagenome-assembled genomes and identifying biosynthetic gene clusters with potential biomedical applications.

In regulatory genomics, I performed sequence-based analysis of promoter regions to identify transcription factor binding patterns, integrating motif discovery with transcriptomic data to construct gene regulatory networks.

Current Direction

My current research interest lies in transitioning from descriptive genomics to predictive modeling. I am particularly interested in how sequence-level patterns can be encoded into machine learning models to predict functional outcomes, including gene regulation and phenotype.

Technical Approach

I work primarily in Python and R, using high-performance computing environments (SLURM) to handle large datasets. My workflows emphasize reproducibility, scalability, and integration across multiple data types including sequence, expression, and functional annotations.
