Research Direction & Vision

My research sits at the intersection of genomics, statistical learning, and multi-omics integration. I am focused on building frameworks that move beyond description using sequence-level features and multi-modal biological data to predict function, resistance, and disease outcomes.

I am currently expanding into single-cell RNA-seq analysis and exploring how deep learning architectures (transformers, graph neural networks) can be applied to biological sequences, targeting drug discovery, oncology, and infectious disease applications where AI is reshaping what is possible.
