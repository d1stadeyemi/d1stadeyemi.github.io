About Me

Research Direction & Vision

My research sits at the intersection of microbial genomics, statistical learning, and multi-omics integration. I am particularly focused on building computational frameworks that move beyond description — using sequence-level features and multi-modal biological data to make predictions about function, resistance, and disease outcomes.

Currently, I am expanding my expertise into single-cell RNA-seq analysis and exploring how deep learning architectures (transformers, graph neural networks) can be applied to biological sequences. I am motivated to contribute to areas where computational biology is reshaping drug discovery, oncology, and infectious disease — building models that not only describe biology but predict and ultimately guide therapeutic decisions.

Metagenomics & Microbiome Bulk & Single-Cell RNA-seq ML / AI for Genomics Drug Discovery AMR Research Regulatory Genomics Multi-Omics Integration Biosynthetic Gene Clusters Predictive Biomarkers HPC & Scalable Pipelines
